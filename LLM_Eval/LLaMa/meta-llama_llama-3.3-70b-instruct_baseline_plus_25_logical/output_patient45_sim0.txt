The hospitalization risk score is calculated based on the individual's health status, family history, and lifestyle factors, with a focus on conditions and behaviors that have a direct causal relationship to increased hospitalization risk, such as depression, sleep deprivation, and genetic predisposition to diabetes. The score reflects the cumulative effect of these factors, weighted by their relative contributions to hospitalization risk.
