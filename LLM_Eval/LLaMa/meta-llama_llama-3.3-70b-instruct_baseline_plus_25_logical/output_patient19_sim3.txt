The hospitalization risk score is calculated based on the individual's depression diagnosis, socioeconomic status, and reported sadness or fatigue, which are significant risk factors, while their age, BMI, employment, and sleep habits contribute to a lower risk. The score reflects the causal relationships between these factors and the likelihood of hospitalization, with a higher score indicating a greater risk.
