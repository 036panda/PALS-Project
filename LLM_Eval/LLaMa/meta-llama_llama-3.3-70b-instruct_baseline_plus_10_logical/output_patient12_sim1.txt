The hospitalization risk score is calculated based on the individual's health status, lifestyle, and socioeconomic factors, with a focus on conditions and behaviors that have a direct causal relationship to increased hospitalization risk, such as age, obesity, smoking, and family health history. The score reflects the cumulative effect of these factors, adjusted for mitigating factors such as adequate sleep and middle socioeconomic status.
