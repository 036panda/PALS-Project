The hospitalization risk score is calculated based on the individual's health and lifestyle factors, with a focus on those that have a direct causal link to increased or decreased risk of hospitalization, such as alcohol intake, sleep hours, and socioeconomic status. The score reflects a balance of these factors, with the individual's moderate alcohol intake and slightly below average sleep hours contributing to a higher risk, while their employment, high socioeconomic status, and lack of depression symptoms reduce their overall risk.
