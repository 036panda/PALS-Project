The hospitalization risk score is calculated based on the cumulative effect of individual risk factors, considering both the presence of health issues and the absence of protective factors, with a focus on established causal relationships between these factors and hospitalization risk. The score reflects the increased vulnerability of the patient due to advanced age, obesity, family health history, and smoking, partially mitigated by the absence of certain symptoms and adequate sleep.
