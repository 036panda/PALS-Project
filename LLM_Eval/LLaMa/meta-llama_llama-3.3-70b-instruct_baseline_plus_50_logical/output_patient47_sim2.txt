The hospitalization risk score is calculated based on the individual's health status, family health history, and lifestyle factors that have a direct causal relationship with increased risk of hospitalization, such as schizophrenia, mild depression, and being overweight. The score reflects the cumulative effect of these factors, adjusted for protective factors like adequate sleep and moderate alcohol intake.
