The hospitalization risk score is calculated based on the presence of factors that directly influence health risks, such as high BMI, smoking, and mental health indicators like anxiety and symptoms of depression, balanced against protective factors like adequate sleep and low alcohol intake. The score reflects a logical and causal assessment of the individual's health risk profile, without reference to population-level baselines.
