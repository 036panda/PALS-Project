The hospitalization risk score is calculated based on the individual's health status, behavioral factors, and socioeconomic conditions, with a focus on depression diagnosis, sadness or fatigue, and low socioeconomic status as primary risk contributors. The score reflects the causal relationships between these factors and the likelihood of hospitalization, with a higher score indicating a greater risk.
