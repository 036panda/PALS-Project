The hospitalization risk score is calculated based on the individual's health factors, including their family health history, current diagnosis, and lifestyle choices, with a focus on the positive contributions of depression symptoms, sleep deprivation, and genetic predisposition, balanced by the negative contributions of moderate alcohol consumption, high socioeconomic status, and stable employment. The score reflects the logical and causal relationships between these factors and the individual's overall risk of hospitalization.
