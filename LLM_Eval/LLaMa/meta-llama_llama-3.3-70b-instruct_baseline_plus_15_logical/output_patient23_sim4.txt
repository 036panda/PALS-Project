The hospitalization risk score is calculated based on the summation of positive and negative contributions from various factors, reflecting both physical health indicators (like BMI and family health history) and mental health indicators (such as current diagnosis of schizophrenia and loss of interest), as well as lifestyle and socioeconomic factors. This approach logically integrates multiple aspects of an individual's life to estimate their risk of hospitalization, considering both direct health factors and indirect indicators of health and well-being.
