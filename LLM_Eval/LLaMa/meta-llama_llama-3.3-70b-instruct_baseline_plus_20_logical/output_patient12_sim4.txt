The hospitalization risk score is calculated based on the individual's health status, lifestyle, and genetic predisposition, with a focus on factors that have a direct causal relationship with increased hospitalization risk, such as advanced age, obesity, smoking, and family health history. The score reflects the cumulative effect of these factors, with some factors, like normal depression score and adequate sleep, contributing to a decreased risk.
