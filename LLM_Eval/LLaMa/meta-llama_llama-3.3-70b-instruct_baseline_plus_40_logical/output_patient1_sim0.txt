The hospitalization risk score is calculated based on the presence of significant health risk factors such as a family history of cancer, current diagnosis of anxiety, severe depression, smoking, and low socioeconomic status, which are known to increase the likelihood of hospitalization. The score reflects the cumulative effect of these factors, adjusted for the individual's relatively young age, healthy BMI, and adequate sleep, which mitigate some of the risk.
