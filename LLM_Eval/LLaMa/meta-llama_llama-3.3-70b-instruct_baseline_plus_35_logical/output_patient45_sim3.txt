The hospitalization risk score is calculated based on the individual's health factors, such as family history of diabetes, current diagnosis of depression, and low sleep hours, which increase the risk of hospitalization, while factors like high socioeconomic status and normal HAM-D score decrease the risk. The score reflects the logical and causal relationships between these factors and hospitalization risk, without assuming any baseline or population-level risk.
