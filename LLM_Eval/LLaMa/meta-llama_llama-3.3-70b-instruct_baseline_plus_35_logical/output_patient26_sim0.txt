The hospitalization risk score is calculated based on the individual's health status, lifestyle, and socioeconomic factors, with a focus on conditions and behaviors that have a direct causal relationship to increased hospitalization risk, such as family history of chronic conditions, current mental health diagnosis, and moderate alcohol intake. The score reflects the cumulative effect of these factors, adjusted for protective factors like adequate sleep, middle socioeconomic status, and absence of smoking and depression.
