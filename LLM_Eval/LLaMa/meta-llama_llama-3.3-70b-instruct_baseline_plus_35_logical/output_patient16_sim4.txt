The hospitalization risk score is calculated based on the individual's health conditions, lifestyle choices, and socioeconomic factors that have a direct causal relationship with increased risk of hospitalization, such as smoking, family history of hypertension, and current diagnosis of anxiety and mild depression. The score reflects the cumulative effect of these factors, adjusted for mitigating factors like adequate sleep and stable employment.
