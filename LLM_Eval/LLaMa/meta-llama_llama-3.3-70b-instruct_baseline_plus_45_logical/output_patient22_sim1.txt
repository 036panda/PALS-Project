The hospitalization risk score is calculated based on the individual's health-related factors, such as age, BMI, and mental health status, which have a direct causal relationship to hospitalization risk, while other factors are not directly related to hospitalization risk and therefore do not contribute to the score. The score reflects the cumulative effect of these factors, with a higher score indicating a higher risk of hospitalization.
