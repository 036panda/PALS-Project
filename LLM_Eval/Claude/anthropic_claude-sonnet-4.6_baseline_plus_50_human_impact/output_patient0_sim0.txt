## 3. Rationale

The combination of a schizophrenia diagnosis and severe depression (HAM-D 25+) represents the dominant drivers of hospitalization risk, as co-occurring psychotic and mood disorders substantially elevate psychiatric crisis vulnerability. Protective lifestyle factors such as employment, regular exercise, and a healthy BMI partially offset this risk, but do not adequately counterbalance the severity of the psychiatric burden compounded by smoking, poor sleep, and low hydration.
