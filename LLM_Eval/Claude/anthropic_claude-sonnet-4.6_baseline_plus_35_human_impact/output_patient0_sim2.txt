## 3. Rationale

The combination of a schizophrenia diagnosis and severe depression (HAM-D 25+) represents a clinically significant psychiatric burden that substantially elevates hospitalization risk, particularly given the presence of anhedonia (loss of interest) and poor sleep. Protective factors such as stable employment, middle socioeconomic status, and an active social life partially offset these risks but are insufficient to counterbalance the severity of the co-occurring psychiatric conditions.
