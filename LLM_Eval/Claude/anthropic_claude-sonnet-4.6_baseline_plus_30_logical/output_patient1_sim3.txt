### Justification

The score is primarily driven by clinically causal factors: a severe HAM-D depression score (25+) combined with an active anxiety diagnosis, smoking, moderate alcohol intake, and low socioeconomic status collectively elevate hospitalization risk substantially, as these are empirically linked to acute mental and physical health crises requiring inpatient care. Protective factors — young age, healthy BMI, vegetable consumption, social engagement (relationship, religion, travel), and the absence of reported sadness or loss of interest despite the HAM-D score — partially offset these risks, while lifestyle and preference variables with no established causal pathway to hospitalization are assigned zero contribution.
