## 3. Rationale

The primary driver of hospitalization risk is the Bipolar diagnosis, which is significantly compounded by chronically insufficient sleep (5.7 hours), a well-established trigger for manic or depressive episodes in this population. Protective factors such as high socioeconomic status, employment, regular exercise, and the absence of current depressive symptoms meaningfully offset risk, but the combination of young age, alcohol use, and sleep disruption keeps vulnerability moderately elevated.
