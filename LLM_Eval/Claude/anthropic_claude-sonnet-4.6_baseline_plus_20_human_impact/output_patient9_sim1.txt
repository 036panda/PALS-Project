## 3. Rationale

The primary drivers of hospitalization risk are the bipolar disorder diagnosis combined with concurrent mild depression symptoms and significant sleep deprivation (5.3 hours), which together can destabilize mood regulation and increase the likelihood of a manic or depressive episode requiring inpatient care. Protective factors such as stable employment, middle socioeconomic status, and healthy lifestyle habits (no smoking, vegetable consumption) partially offset these risks but do not fully mitigate the vulnerability created by the psychiatric and sleep-related concerns.
