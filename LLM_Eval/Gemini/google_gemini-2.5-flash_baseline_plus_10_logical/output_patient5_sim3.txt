The hospitalization risk score is calculated by summing the individual contributions of factors known to causally influence health outcomes and hospitalization likelihood, with positive values indicating increased risk and negative values indicating decreased risk. Factors like age, family history of chronic disease, and insufficient sleep are assigned positive contributions due to their established links to adverse health, while healthy behaviors and socioeconomic advantages are assigned negative contributions.
