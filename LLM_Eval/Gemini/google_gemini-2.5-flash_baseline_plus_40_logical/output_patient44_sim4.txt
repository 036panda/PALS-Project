The risk score is calculated by summing the numerical contributions of factors causally linked to hospitalization risk, with positive values indicating increased risk and negative values indicating reduced risk. Factors such as advanced age, low socioeconomic status, lack of exercise, and certain health conditions or predispositions are assigned positive contributions due to their established impact on health outcomes and healthcare utilization. Conversely, protective factors like not smoking and adequate sleep are assigned negative contributions.
