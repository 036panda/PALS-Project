The hospitalization risk score is calculated by summing the contributions of factors directly impacting physical and mental health, with particular emphasis on chronic conditions, lifestyle choices, and mental health indicators known to increase the likelihood of requiring medical intervention. Factors like moderate depression, chronic sleep deprivation, and a family history of asthma are assigned positive contributions due to their established causal links to increased health complications and hospitalization risk, while positive health behaviors like exercising and not smoking are assigned negative contributions.
