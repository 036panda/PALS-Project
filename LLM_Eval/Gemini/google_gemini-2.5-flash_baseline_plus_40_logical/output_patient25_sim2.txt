The risk score is calculated by summing the contributions of factors known to causally influence hospitalization risk, such as age, obesity, family history of serious illness, and existing severe mental health diagnoses, while subtracting for the absence of major risk factors like smoking. Factors with no direct or strong causal link to hospitalization are assigned a neutral contribution.
