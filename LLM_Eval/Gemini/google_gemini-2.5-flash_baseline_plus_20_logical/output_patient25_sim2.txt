The risk score is calculated by summing the individual contributions of factors directly and causally linked to hospitalization risk, such as age, BMI, family history, and current diagnoses, while subtracting contributions from protective factors like not smoking and adequate sleep. Each contribution reflects a direct causal link to either increasing or decreasing the likelihood of requiring hospitalization.
