The risk score is calculated by summing the contributions of factors known to causally influence hospitalization risk, such as chronic conditions, mental health diagnoses, lifestyle choices, and socioeconomic determinants, while subtracting contributions from protective factors. The logical basis is that each factor either directly increases the likelihood of needing hospital care or contributes to overall health resilience.
