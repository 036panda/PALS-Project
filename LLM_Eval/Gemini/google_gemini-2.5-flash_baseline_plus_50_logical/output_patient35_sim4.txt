The risk score is calculated by summing the contributions of factors that either increase or decrease the likelihood of hospitalization, based on established medical and public health correlations. Positive contributions are assigned to known risk factors (e.g., chronic conditions, unhealthy habits, insufficient sleep, allergies), while negative contributions are assigned to protective factors (e.g., good mental health, exercise, high socioeconomic status, absence of major risk factors).
