**Justification:**

The hospitalization risk score is calculated by summing the individual contributions of various health, lifestyle, and demographic factors. Positive contributions are assigned to factors known to increase hospitalization risk (e.g., depression, insufficient sleep, racial disparities), while negative contributions are assigned to protective factors (e.g., not smoking, employment). The severity of depression, family history of asthma, and racial disparities are weighted more heavily due to their established causal links to increased healthcare utilization and adverse health outcomes.
