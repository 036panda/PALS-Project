The risk score is calculated by summing the contributions of factors known to causally influence health outcomes and healthcare utilization, such as age, smoking, severe sleep deprivation, and socioeconomic status, while assigning zero contribution to factors without a direct causal link. Each contribution reflects the established impact of the factor on the likelihood of requiring hospitalization.
