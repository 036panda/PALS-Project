The risk score is calculated by summing the contributions of factors directly and causally linked to increased or decreased hospitalization risk, such as smoking, family history of chronic disease, and current mental health diagnoses, while mitigating factors like employment and healthy eating habits reduce the score. Factors without a clear causal link to hospitalization risk are assigned a neutral contribution.
