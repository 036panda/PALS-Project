The risk score is calculated by summing the contributions of factors directly and causally linked to hospitalization risk, such as age, BMI, family history of chronic disease, poor sleep, and lack of exercise, while mitigating factors like not smoking reduce the score. Factors unrelated to health outcomes or those with no current impact (e.g., current diagnosis of depression but normal HAM-D) are assigned a neutral contribution.
