**Justification:**

The hospitalization risk score is calculated by summing the individual contributions of various health and lifestyle factors. Positive contributions are assigned to factors that increase the likelihood of hospitalization (e.g., age, overweight BMI, depression symptoms, poor sleep, allergies, unhealthy eating habits), while negative contributions are assigned to protective factors (e.g., middle socioeconomic status, exercise). The sum of these causally linked contributions directly yields the final risk score.
