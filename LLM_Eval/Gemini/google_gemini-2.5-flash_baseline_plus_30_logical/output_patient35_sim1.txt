**Justification:**

The risk score is calculated by summing the individual contributions of factors known to either increase or decrease hospitalization likelihood, with a strong emphasis on direct health conditions, lifestyle choices, and socioeconomic determinants. Positive contributions are assigned to factors that elevate risk (e.g., chronic diagnoses, poor sleep, family history), while negative contributions are assigned to protective factors (e.g., high socioeconomic status, no smoking, good mental health indicators).
